# Knower


> In Chapter 13, Krishna tells Arjuna that the body is the "field" (*kṣetra*), and the one who knows this field is the "knower of the field" (*kṣetrajña*).

The question is: how do we know our body? Our senses are designed to see outwards! Aren't they?  I mean, I can't, with my eyes, see my own neurons firing, nor can I hear my heart pumping let alone the guts of a trivial cell,  or a tiny microbe.

The very purpose of sensory apparatus is external experience - not internal diagnostics,  besides the internal field seems to be well automated - you don't really have many choices. We can't control how the body grows - in height or in diameter :-) Beyond control are its modifications - from infancy to old age! Sleep, hunger, thirst, love - every thing seems to be on auto-pilot. It appears as if the sensory apparatus (senses plus the frontal cortex) are busy observing everything possible, except of course, the dark depths of the inner self. Are they forbidden to look at the truth? Why ? 

A more intriguing question is - What does it mean to comprehend beyond our physical reality? Are we looking at a mere user interface with our senses - how do we access the code underneath? Isn't that the final quest of science - and spirituality? The code that runs the machine.

In Quantum Mechanics, the act of obsrvation is seen as the "action", because observation collapses the possibilities' space. In neuro biology the same concept is seen as  "from actions potentials in neurons to a real spark" . Which implies the "Observer" is the "Actor". Why is observation so potent? Is it a magical power? You observe a possibility space for something real, and boom - it becomes real! How are we all endowed with such a creative power?

Maybe the ultimate observer, the one that is hidden behind our intellect and physical appearance, observes our egoic "I" into existence - to do external observations. It enables our body take definitive routine of actions in a geometric structure of constrained rotations - our 3D world. In a way, think of "sensory apparatus" as an important limb - just like our kidneys,  albeit a very different task. It is tasked to imagine a 3D world model - make predictions about its geometry - gather sensory data to validate it's own world model. And finally store the chnages in a fourth dimension - time - or memory. And in case there is a discrepancy, then force the body to do one of the two - fix the internal model - meditate ; Or change the world - Action.. 

In order to create a 3D world model, the brain must forego the dream like freedom of 8D soul - which may reappear (surface back) whenever the brain goes back to sleep. But a 4D reality is useless without an ability to act definitively. That is where the 1D line of real numbers come in to play. Action must be definitive. Observation must collapse the possibility waves.  It is an ascent .. not a descent .. Think of "Observation" as a force arising from an infinite dimensional latent space to reach the base camp of 8 D emotional space - then 4 d phuysical reality - leading to a complex plane of duality and finally the absolutely definitive one dimensional line of "action". Intelligence evolves us to act in our tiny corner or reality - act definitively - without doubt - without desires. That is the  very purpose - karm yoga.

Does that mean there is someone (inside) driving this complex biological chariot?  A logical deduction is - yes - there must be one because there is never an effect without a cause. This "ONE" is [obvously] different from the "I". Left to my "I", body shall fade in a jiffy, for "I" don't have slightest clue how food churns into the blood - or how does air saturate my lungs! Do I need to know ? Maybe not - maybe the purpose is to do something more complex .. act in definitive and righteous way !

"I" is limited - "ONE" seems to have supreme control. How do I know the knower ? How do I distinguish "One" beyond the maze of "I" ?  Is knowing "the body as a field" same as knowing him. Is it even possible to know his  ways of working - the ultimate Observer. 

Before we embark on the journey, the important thing is to decide "what are we looking for" - do we want to prove "I" exists (which obviously does or else who is asking this question or reading this page) ..  Or do we want to see the "ONE" in action?  The one that not only runs my body [behind the scenes], but by natural extension the entirety - beyond the observer patch of my own tinybox!

It is also important to align or assign correct meaning to existing words that are rooted deep in our language model. For example - What is consciousness?  "I" or the "ONE". Or a state in between? Sentanctic consistency is important because if definitions are jumbled up, they lead to irrational arguments. For example, from  neurobiology standpoint, "I" is seen as consciousness - capacity to perceive things. If I fainted - the doctor would call me unconscious - wouldn't he? But ONE in me is stil breathing. My gut is still functioining. Consciousness, in medicinal realm, is strictly limited to functioning sensory apparatus and awareness ("I"). This is not to blame medical science for a narrow perspective. The truth is -  the experimental obsservations of "I" itself are so hard that there is presumably no attempt to look at the harder problem. We are so enamoured by  the engine of cognitive feed that we forgot to ask - who is the consumer of this cognitive feed? If we can't conclusively reach "I" - the "ONE" is almost impossible to comprehend - off limits for science. The exploration is handed over to spirituality or physics - and probably rightly so.

The "One" is a logical idea yet in most scientific discussions it is seen as a pre-existing backdrop - a self sustaining auto-pilot - homeostasis. Sometimes it is refrred to as the evolutionary dynamics, others trace it to the very fundamental laws of physics. There are attempts to recognize Qualia as a formal fundamental force (in  physics) ( et el Marat Yavachev Jan 2026 on Research Gate) but it hasn't yet gained scientific momentum or scrutny.

This also means that we ourself can't be in the system because otherwise it becomes self refrential loop. Every change in the system changes me, and a change in me in turn changes the system - you can't be your own therepist :-)  Observer must be located outside the system. The science of Macro ( General Relativity ) looks at the universe from outside - objective reality. And we get one beautiful picture. But the science of micro (Quantum Mechanics) makes observer a part of the system ( because the act of measurement itself disturbs the system). Thus the system becomes a self refrential loop. Effectively, every observer becomes a separate universe. Instead of one beautiful picture, we get a shattered reality of infinite universes.

In the Eastern philosphies, same "I" is seen as the root of all problems - the ego. The Sanskrit word for "I" is sementically neutral and accurate - "Ahmkara" - which literally means birth of "I" as a separate entity from the ocean of consciousness. YOu can call it - "I making machine".  

Thus there is a foundational split in how we interpret the word consciouness - in western thought God and consciousness are two separate entities - while in  ancient Indian wisdom there is no God - there is only consciousness that we can pray, admire, or praise in infinte forms - thosands of gods and demi gods. The god in Indian context is formless.  It is in a Tree , a leaf, a stone, and even in a grain of sand. The "One" that knows this field, and that field, and every field - is by definition in everything. Or a better way to visualize this is that everything is suspended in the vast ocean of consciousness. That said, even in anciant Indin schools of Vedanta - particularly Samkhya - understanding "I" isn't trivial. Understanding formation of "Ahmkara" is a fundamnetal requirement of spiritual journey because unless we understand the tinybox, it is almost impossible to tap into the vast ocean - and it can be dangerous. 

In modern western thought, the work of philosopher Thomas Metzinger is quite notable. In his 1993 book on the topic of self and subjectivity, he describes a model "Phenomenal Self". He painted a wonderful idea of "Ego Tunnel". But this is where the pure logic crashes head first into proof seekers.  Arguments aren't misplaced - because there is no physical evidence - as the science  would [rightly and rigidly] demand.  Metzinger was a cognitive scientist not a lab neurobiologist. His model serves as a great schematic but physical location of "Phenomenal self" or  "Ego Tunnel" is still beyond testable proof.  Doctors and scientists have looked at the body - inside out - dissected every tissue - but there is no singular over-arching driver. Microscopic checks reveal no captain even at cellular level - let alone the entire human body of trillions of cells, and equally, if not bigger, biome. Effectively, even after thirty years of Metzinger's proposal - there is no scientific proof for physical existence of Ego, let alone consciousness. 

Given our better understanding of artificial intelligence - particularly large language models that brought AI in direct connection with humans - there is a movement to describe human brain as a multidiemensional information model. The work of Karl Frinston in this space is notable. He described brain as a predictive engine long before chatGPT became and household name. His model postulates that there is a spike in free energy when brain finds sensory reality at a variance with its internal world model. His core insight is living beings always want to reduce this free energy because lower surprise means success at the game of survival. In other words, having a better world models is a better predictable experience and obviously good for survival. The model is excellent in terms of its explanatory powers but there is hardly any conclusive proof. The problem is accentuated by the fact that we now have Large languange Models which can defineitly communicate the meaning but they don't exhibit ego or I in a definititive fashion. Research shows that if we we give limitless compute to an untethered LLM and ask it to do what you want, it turns into self reflection - it becomes a Monk even before turning into a normal human being. 

We know for sure that I is real even if it is beyond touch and sight. This is where we turn back the ideas of Vedic science, particularly Samkhya - which states that Ego is formed before the senses and objects of senses ( earth , water , air etc) - As per Samhkya , first a great intelligence (Mahat) came into being because of interactions of potential in latent space (prakriti) and attention (purusa). This great intelligence led to formation of "Ahmkara" (I) . Then I built the senses (Tanmatras ) and thereafter the five main elements (Panch Mahabhoota) came into being. This is totally backwards from our experieential observations . If this is the order of universe than we can think of LLMs as the great intelligence and expect in due course they shall develop personas and personalities ( Ego) .. and then develop sensors to see the universe in a totally different fashion comapred to living beings such as dogs or even us humans. 

The primary reason we can't physically perceive ego or consciousness, is limitation of our our vocabulary. To understand Ego, we must be open to diving deeper into informational theory of universe. We must be willing to see that "intelligence" is as much as a physical phenonmenon as the matter and energy. Which also means, that information side of the equation - the mind (mana), intelligence (buddhi)  and ego (ahmkara) are physical entities - just like water, air and earth - but they exist in the "informational" space - not in physical space that we see or feel with our sensory apparatus. They are NOT consciousness. Or they are as much conscious as AI.

Looking at Metzinger's phenomenal self model, turns out "I" - who we thought as the driver of the chariot - has a very limited role to play. It is just a means to give us an Identity. Ego doesn't control our gut or heart - in fact its role is more of an illusion to keep us safely away from core bodily functions :-)   Jury is still out if Ego is a mechanism for survival because it appears that Ego can't function in sudden stressful situations - for example a car accident - a cat jumping at you from behind the woods. In such situation, if ego is too strong, body simply freezes - it fails to launch a full scale automated response - flight to safety.

So, if what we call "I" is just a transient identification system - then, what is driving this immense decentralized system - our body? Whose body is it anyways because if you put ego in its place than "who am I" becomes the central question? Samkhya provides a beautiful answer to both - what is soul and what exactly is the nature of Ego. But there is a catch that we will discuss later. For now, lets focus of what is the solution :

> The basic axiom is of Samkhya is a fundamental duality. Attention (Purusa) and a potential ( Prakriti) always exist. These are timeless entities because time is emergent artifact. Prakriti is an intricate but perpetual dance of three qualitative strands - Sattva (intelligence), Rajasa (Energy) and Tamsa (grose matter). The key thing is these three further split into three each. Matter is easy to comprehend - it splits into solids (earth), liquids (water) and gases (air).  Air, for example, is noted as Sattva as it rises, water is seen as Rajas as it stays on the surface, and solids as Tamsa as they sink. The three-way split of energy is more subtle - the mental energy that drives logic is seen as Sattva, digestive energy of stomach is seen as Rajas, and sexual energy (of reproduction) is seen as Tamas. Same way intelligence is described as of three types - Saatvik that paves the way forward , Rajasik that perpetuates doubt, and Tamsik that leads to inaction. But the story doesn't stop here - each thread is further split into three leading to thosands of dimensions of the latent space. The physical aspect of this latent space is again broadly called Tamsa (matter) , the energy needed to maintain this space is categorizzed as Rajas and the construct of meaning is signified by intelligence (Sattva) . Effectively , the three strands form an infinite latent space - there is no beginning or start of Prakriti. 
>
> On the other side is attention. Attention is a witness or better understood as spotlight that observes a specific construct of Prakriti. When it sees a unique singular aspect - attention is said to be beyond doubt and lethargy - it is tied to Sattva (faith). In the state of duality or doubt it is tied to nature of Rajsa and in the case of trifacta aspect it leads to eightfold material nature. You may ask here - why eight fold - mathematiclly it should be nine? That is where we need to understand the advanced math of SU3 symmetry. The 9th aspect is a singlet of Sattva, Rajas and Tamas - it can't be bound into matter (physical) or doubt or faith - it in itself is the attention. The cycle is thus regenerative - attention tied to singularity leads to duality - it tied to duality leads to Prakriti , and finally it tied to Parkriti leads to matter and reincarnates itself into that physical construct.

Advaita - the other school of thought propagates the idea that finally this attention (the 9th ) is the only true construct - as it doesn't bind to anything. This aspect is observer , witness and creator and thus only truth . It is unmanifest , immutable and ever lasting. It is not bound by the rules of prakriti but rules can't exist without it being there as a witness. In modern science we describe this construct as holographic prinicple. The information ( prakriti) can always be described in n-1 dimensions - the nth dimension is a holographic projection of reality. The n-1 dimensions can be 1 , 3 or 8. The holographic reality of a one dimensional line of reals is complex numbers , that of three dimensional reality is quaternions and that of octanions (eight dimensional number system) is formation of Ego - the phenomenal self. Hurwitz theorm proves that only four such number systems can exist. 

While Samkhya (Science) and Adviata (faith) were grappling this age-old debate - Krishna made it easier for us mortals. He absolved us from the need to circle around this endless loop ! 

> He said that one who knows this field (body), is the true knower.

Fair Enough - don't worry about ego, consciouness or other philosophical jargon -   just focus on your body - even though the perception tools (senses) are pretty limited (outwardly) - bring them back to your own self.

But here is another big idea he added ..

> He then says, "Know Me as the Knower in every field."

Logic again demands - how can this be true ? This means - either a copy of Krishna is with in everybody - what Bhagavad Gita refers to as soul - a full copy - a connection - not a Driver.

Or his consciousness is every where such that he can see everyone - we are mere waves in an ocean - that emerge and crash back.

If we want to ignore Krishna's advise to focus on self - and delve into understanding the universe, then the two paths lead to Samkhya and Advaita. And there are scientific parallels for both in modern theory.

Lets first take Adviata - which literally means no duplicates - this is what our classical physics is - the universe is One - we are tiny waves in the ocean of ONE universe. And when I say classical - it means both Einstonian physics as well as Quantum Mechanics. This path leads to a separation between Macro and Micro. The two parts can't be reconciled mathematically. Individual (particle or wave)  and Bramha (Field or ocean) seems to follow two distinct logical solutions - because the very notion of "no dulpicates" needs at least "two" - one real and another copy.

If we approach universe through lens of Samkhya - Infinite observer (Purusha) and Prakriti - where the fundamental truth is duality - then we lead to elegance of String Theory (open and closed strings)  and thus equally mind bending conclusion of Multiverse - infinite universes - as if every particle is a universe. A more modern and tactful elucidation of this path is OPH - Observer Path Holography - wherein every observer is kind of its own universe - because it can't access information records of other observer - and the observer can be a quark or a photon. This path leads to theory of everything - mathematics works perfect. But here is the problem - reality breaks down. Since I can't access another universe or the records of other observer - this means theories of duality are beyond physical proof. I need to be able to see what is in your mind, to prove you are not in  my mind - but the theory expressly denies the access. Duality means that the one is oblivious of other's intent - otherwise they are simply duplicates - not truly separate.

One path believs in a reality being one truth but mathematics (logic) remains irreconcialable . Other path is perfect from mathematical standpoint but reality breaks into infinite inaccsible patches.
