# Knower


> In Chapter 13, Krishna said the body is the "field" (*kṣetra*), and the one who knows this field is the "knower of the field" (*kṣetrajña*).

The question is: how do we know our body? Our senses are designed to see outwards! Aren't they?  I mean, I can't, with my eyes, see my own neurons firing, nor can I hear my heart pumping, let alone the guts of a trivial cell,  or those of a tiny microbe.

> The very purpose of sensory apparatus is external experience - not internal diagnostics,  besides the internal field seems to be fully automated. 

When it comes to autonomy,  we don't really have many choices. We can't control how the body grows - in height or in diameter :-) Beyond control are its modifications - from infancy to the passing! Sleep, hunger, thirst, love - every thing seems to be on auto-pilot. The apparatus (senses plus the frontal cortex)  is always busy observing external stimulii as much as  possible - doing its intended, though complicated, task. The only point of conflict is - does it listen to the subtle clues of body ? Does it focus on internal valance ? The good news is - internal stuff doesn't take much bandwidth. It is not an extra effort for the brain - it is the act of resting - simply stopping observations for a moment - not willfully - as a part of rythmic flow of a moment.

Traditional science is silent about the observer except the acknowledgement that Observer is a fundamental necessity to collapse the potent possibilities (often represented by a wave function). On the other hand, traditional  spiritual argument is - should we aspire for more of the physical stuff (decohered potential) when the quest is really to understand the infinite potential - the vast sea of consciousness!  One is focused on decohered potential (physical reality), other is on a "devotional chase" of sum total of all possibilities - what about the observer? 

Why would evolution ( or God)  design us the way we are - chasing either survival (and or physical growth) or seeking escaspe from the reality -  while ignoring the self - the seer - the observer. Krishna resolved this fundamental paradox - he said that the body is worth knowing - it is the field. You are the observer. The sensory apparatus and actions organs together give us agency.

Body is our means to act in this world. Without the inputs of the sensory organs  and the motion of action organs, it is impossible to navigate this reality. Bhagavad Gita, being the very source of Karm Yoga, sees the body as the primary field of action. Scientifically speaking, our senses allow us into most exquisite user interface ever designed. Not only can I see the three dimensional movie playing, I am part of it. I am actively carving out my own reality. My actions make or break my life - and that of many others around me. Our collective helluciantion gives rise to the reality.

That brings up the question - what exactly is action?  Knowing the body and the mind is all about understanding the intricacies and origin of physical actins. How do they shapes the reality around us. As we said, in Quantum physics, the act of obsrvation is seen as the "action", because observation collapses the possibilities' space. In neuro biology the same concept is put in a different form - "from actions potentials to a real sparks in subsets of neurons - each mapping a unique possibility". Which implies the "Observer" is the "Actor". Why is observation so potent? Is it a magical power? You observe a possibility space, and boom - it becomes real! How and why are we all endowed with such a creative potency? 

But observation alone is not sufficient to manifest the reality - other observers must agree with your observations - reality is a shared hellucination - consensus. If one neuron fires, brain probably wont even notice , but when thousads light up in a group , it becomes a concept. Think of "sensory apparatus" as another organ, just like our kidneys, albeit a very different task. It imagines a 3D world model. Most of the time it stays in its own 3 D world  but every now and then it interacts with other models - the overlap creates reality. At its core - the shared reality is the 3 D visual we can touch and feel - at abstract level the shared reality is the language we used to express the reality.


In order to create a 3D world model, the brain must forego the dream like freedom of 8D soul - which may reappear (surface back) whenever the brain goes back to sleep. But a 4D reality is useless without an ability to act definitively. That is where the 1D line of real numbers come in to play. Action must be definitive. Observation must collapse the possibility waves.  It is an ascent .. not a descent .. Think of "Observation" as a force arising from an infinite dimensional latent space to reach the base camp of 8 D emotional space - then 4 d phuysical reality - leading to a complex plane of duality and finally the absolutely definitive one dimensional line of "action". Intelligence evolves us to act in our tiny corner or reality - act definitively - without doubt - without desires. That is the  very purpose - karm yoga.
