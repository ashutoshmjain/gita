# Knower


In Chapter 13, Krishna tells Arjuna that the body is the "field" (*kṣetra*), and the one who knows this field is the "knower of the field" (*kṣetrajña*). The question is: how do we know our body? Our senses are designed to see outwards! Aren't they?  I mean, I can't, with my own eyes, see my neurons firing, nor can I hear my heart pumping. 

The purpose of sensory apparatus is external experience - not internal diagnostics. And the internal field seems to be well automated - we can't control how it grows - in height or in diameter :-) Beyond control are its modifications from infancy to old age! Sleep, hunger, thirst, love - every thing seems to be on auto-pilot. Does that mean there is someone inside driving this complex biological chariot? A logical deduction is - yes - there must be one because there is never an effect without a cause. "I" is a logical idea.  Important thing to note here - "I" is neither God nor Soul  - it is "Phenomenal Ego Self" - cite Metzinger's PSM. Or simply Ego - or Ahmkara in Vedic literature - the notion of "I" as the doer.

But this is where the logic crashes head first into proof seekers.  Arguments aren't misplaced - because there is no physical evidence - as the science  would [rightly] demand. Doctors and scientists have looked at the body - inside out - dissected every part of every tissue - but there is no one singular over-arching driver to be found. Even microscopic checks reveal no captain at cellular level - let alone the entire human body of trillions of cells. Ultimately, it gets into realm of unproven hard problems - is "I" fundamental or is it emergent? The problem is - we know it exists - we feel being alive! Should we assume there is no singular ME running this complex system - it is simply on collective auto-pilot! If there is no "I" , then there has to be something - even if it is a fully self driving car - there has to be some sort of intelligence - artificial or biological or spiritul. Even evolutionary chaos or randomness has a logic to it !

While Samkhya (Science) and Adviata (faith) were grappling this age-old debate - Krishna made it easier for us mortals. He absolved us from the need to circle in this endless loop !

> He said that one who knows this field (body), is the true knower.

Fair Enough - don't worry about ego, consciouness or other philosophical jargon -   just focus on your body - even though the perception tools (senses) are pretty limited (outwardly) - bring them back to your own self.

But here is another big idea he added ..

> He then says, "Know Me as the Knower in every field."

Logic again demands - how can this be true ? This means - either a copy of Krishna is with in everybody - what Bhagavad Gita refers to as soul - a full copy - a connection - not a Driver.

Or his consciousness is every where such that he can see everyone - we are mere waves in an ocean - that emerge and crash back.

If we want to ignore Krishna's advise to focus on self - and delve into understanding the universe, then the two paths lead to Samkhya and Advaita. And there are scientific parallels for both in modern theory.

Lets first take Adviata - which literally means no duplicates - this is what our classical physics is - the universe is One - we are tiny waves in the ocean of ONE universe. And when I say classical - it means both Einstonian physics as well as Quantum Mechanics. This path leads to a separation between Macro and Micro. The two parts can't be reconciled mathematically. Individual (particle or wave)  and Bramha (Field or ocean) seems to follow two distinct logical solutions - because the very notion of "no dulpicates" needs at least "two" - one real and another copy.

If we approach universe through lens of Samkhya - Infinite observer (Purusha) and Prakriti - where the fundamental truth is duality - then we lead to elegance of String Theory (open and closed strings)  and thus equally mind bending conclusion of Multiverse - infinite universes - as if every particle is a universe. A more modern and tactful elucidation of this path is OPH - Observer Path Holography - wherein every observer is kind of its own universe - because it can't access information records of other observer - and the observer can be a quark or a photon. This path leads to theory of everything - mathematics works perfect. But here is the problem - reality breaks down. Since I can't access another universe or the records of other observer - this means theories of duality are beyond physical proof. I need to be able to see what is in your mind, to prove you are not in  my mind - but the theory expressly denies the access. Duality means that the one is oblivious of other's intent - otherwise they are simply duplicates - not truly separate.

One path believs in a reality being one truth but mathematics (logic) remains irreconcialable . Other path is perfect from mathematical standpoint but reality breaks into infinite inaccsible patches.

The question however is - do I want to understand the universe and individual through mathematics or experience? Do I truly want to  enjoy this wonderful life? Is the goal "knowing" everything or JUST - knowing yourself :-)  Even though - at the end, they both may mean the same - but the important question is which path I want to take.

I think the later is important. 

If that is the I want to take - then here are the three principles of Krishna - super simple to follow and they show immediate results - through experience.

1) Bhakti - Devotion - Always stay focused on Krishna. Truly believe that without his grace it is impossible to navigate this ocean.

2) Karma - Do your duty like there is no tomorrow. Understand your role and work hard with enthusiasm - always.

3) Jnana - Knowledge - Bhakti and Karma open the door to knowledge. Btw - this can be applied to science as well. The scientist who figure out the theories also do so through devotion and action. But Krishna's technology isn't limited to science. The door opens even if you are doing petty chores. It is not the nature of action - it is the action itself. When done with devotion and consistent enthusiasm - even the petty actions lead to true knowledge. That knowledge is bliss. A simple shop keeper or a soldier or a cobbler - all can open the door of knowledge. Don't chase acts of others - focus purely on your actions - dictated self to immediate reality - your tiny box - your observer's patch - your own universe.
