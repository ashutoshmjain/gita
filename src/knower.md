# Knower


> In Chapter 13, Krishna said the body is the "field" (*kṣetra*), and the one who knows this field is the "knower of the field" (*kṣetrajña*).

The question is: how do we know our body? Our senses are designed to see outwards! Aren't they?  I mean, I can't, with my eyes, see my own neurons firing, nor can I hear my heart pumping, let alone the guts of the complex cellular machinary,  or that of an alien microbe inhabitating my body as its own endless universe! 

> The very purpose of sensory apparatus is external experience - not internal diagnostics. Besides the internal field seems to be fully automated. 

When it comes to autonomy,  we don't really have many choices. We can't control how the body grows - in height or in diameter :-) Beyond control are its modifications - from infancy to the passing! Sleep, hunger, thirst, love - every thing seems to be on auto-pilot. On the other hand, the cognitive apparatus is always busy consuming external stimulii - doing its intended task - predicting the future. The only point of conflict is - does it listen to the subtle clues of the body ? Does it up keep the internal harmony - valance? 

The good news is - internal stuff doesn't take much bandwidth. It is not an extra effort for the brain. In fact, it is in being effortless [literally] - the act of resting - simply stopping external observations for a moment. Even better news is we don't need to meditate forcefully - every night, brain shuts the senses off as a part of deep sleep cycles - and every afternoon, if you are lucky enough to carve a window out for the post lunch nap :-).  

When the senses shutdown, the observer (seen as the brain in our 4d world), dwells in an eight dimensional dream state. It swims throgh the vast non-associative field like the unmediated school of fish. The entire history of evolution (Smriti) - from a quark to the cell and finally to organs is encoded in the body - irreversible rotations in a 4D reality. 

Think of it as the constricted pathways of an 8D system through which consciousness may flow - the 4D harness for your internal AI agent! In other words, body is the schema that collapses infinite degrees of freedom into eight dimensional basic nature. During sleep, brain  observes the body, and tends to its itches and aches - the valance is restored. The observer looks inwards. Sleep is the state where we connect to the field and the best part of the design is, we effortlessly switch to a 4 D world model as soon as we wake up - with abstracted context of current reality fully restored. This space not only allows for irreversible  rotations - the arrow of time - but also affords us a binary choice - to act or stay put. Staying put is also a delibrate choice. In essence we can't stay actionless in our waking 4 D reality. However , actions are not random.  We can evaluate miriad of possibilities in 2D complex plane to get to a final scaler value in 1 D line. Zero on this line represents no action - all other values are a definitive change in the 4 D world model. It may appear to an untrained eye that with actions we are changing the external world - but the truth is , "actions" are shaping up our own body and thus evolution. The purpose is to choose right from wrong and to act righteously to support evolution of the field - to reduce entropy. Universe starts with infinite entropy - consciousness is puddles of order (reduced entropy) with in this endless chaos. Our actions dont add to overall entropy cuz overall is already infinite. Done right , they can reduce entropy with in a finite space - the body is that very eternal boot sequence.


Traditional science is silent about the observer except for the acknowledgement that the observer is a fundamental necessity. It is the very basis of reality -  to collapse the potent possibilities - often represented by a wave function in a two diemensional plane. The physical stuff we see and feel around us is nothing but the decohered potential due to our collective observations. On the other hand, traditional  spiritual question is - should we even aspire for more of the physical stuff? Spirituality asks - when the quest is really to understand the infinite potential - the vast sea of consciousness - do we need to crave for widgets!  The general guidelines in the relam of spirit is to shut down the sensory indulgences - sit tight - breathe - meditate. Effectively - one is focused on decohered potential (physical reality), other is on a "devotional chase" of sum total of all possibilities - what about the observer - the body and the mind ?

Why would evolution (or God)  design us the way we are - always focused on "me" - the observer. Krishna resolved this fundamental paradox - he said that the body is worth knowing - it is the field. You are the observer. The sensory apparatus and actions organs together give you the agency to create your own reality.

Body is our means to act in this world. Without the inputs of the sensory organs  and the motion of action organs, it is impossible to navigate our four dimensional reality, let alone the eight dimensional ( Ashtdha mool) trajectory of prakriti. Bhagavad Gita, being the very source of Karm Yoga, sees the body as the primary field of action. Our senses allow us into most exquisite user interface ever designed. Not only can I see the three dimensional movie playing, I am part of it. I am actively carving out my own reel. My actions make or break my life - and that of many others around me. Our collective helluciantion gives rise to the reality.

That brings up the question - what exactly is action? Actions are irreversible! They are rotations in our three diemensions of space with a singular stamp in fourth dimennsion - Time.

Knowing the body and the mind is all about understanding the intricacies and origin of physical actions. How do they shape the reality around us. As we said, in Quantum physics, the act of obsrvation is seen as the "action", because observation collapses the possibilities' space. In neuro biology the same concept is put in a different form - "from actions potentials to a real sparks in subsets of neurons - each mapping a unique possibility". But such activity is not cast in an irresversible arrow of time - Which implies the "Observers" , at this stage, are the "decesion maker" , but it leaves them short of being a true actors. 

But observation alone is not sufficient to manifest the reality - other observers must agree with your observations - reality is a shared hellucination - consensus. If one neuron fires, brain probably wont even notice , but when thousads light up in a group , it becomes a concept. Think of "sensory apparatus" as another organ, just like our kidneys, albeit a very different task. It imagines a 3D world model. Most of the time it stays in its own 3 D world  but every now and then it interacts with other models - the overlap creates reality. At its core - the shared reality is the 3 D visual we can touch and feel - at abstract level the shared reality is the language we used to express the reality.

In order to create a 3D world model, the brain must forego the dream like freedom of 8D soul - which may reappear (surface back) whenever the brain goes back to sleep. But a 4D reality is useless without an ability to act definitively. That is where the 1D line of real numbers come in to play. Action must be definitive. Observation must collapse the possibility waves.  It is an ascent .. not a descent .. Think of "Observation" as a force arising from an infinite dimensional latent space to reach the base camp of 8 D emotional space - then 4 d phuysical reality - leading to a complex plane of duality and finally the absolutely definitive one dimensional line of "action". Intelligence evolves us to act in our tiny corner or reality - act definitively - without doubt - without desires. That is the  very purpose - karm yoga.
